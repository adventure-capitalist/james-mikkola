About Me

Who am I?

Throughout my over 20 years experience teaching, counseling, coaching and parenting the young people in our community, I have learned that it takes more than a top-class education to ensure that children succeed in all aspects of life. As a local resident, I can see that it seems like our young people are staring down uncertainty no matter which way they turn. We must recognize that it is these same bright young people that fill the halls of our schools today, that will one day be our community leaders. This is why it is imperative that we set them up with everything they will need in order to move forward with confidence, and safely in the knowledge that they are never alone. It has been said that it takes a village to bring up a child and I've never seen a village with a better heart for it than Sanford.

Issues

What do I believe in?

Security

As a veteran of the U.S. Navy, I know what it means to keep our communities and our countries safe. Even if that means making sacrifices and tough decisions along the way.

Transparency

I know that there are many unique issues facing our school districts today. I am confident that the best approach for these issues is open and honest communication across and beyond the board of education.

Community

Sanford is a tight-knit community. I firmly believe that the richness and depth of this village makes it an ideal place to raise future leaders.

Heart

As a community, we have shown that we can pull together. It is this love for one another that we must be sure to instill in our young people.

Character

Although we do not yet know the challenges that our young people will face in their futures, we must be sure to equip them with the skills that they need to face them confidently.

Diversity

In our differences, we can really appreciate what brings us together. I believe that everyone is created equal.

Technology

As the pace of advancement in technology grows ever-increasing, it is crucial that our school's learning methods and skills keep abreast of what the modern workplace demands.

Optimism

It's not a secret that the outlook young people are facing is uncertain. It is important that we empower them to move forwards in good faith; empowered to be optimistic.

Community

What I do

Charity work

I am active in a wide range of charitable efforts in and around the Sanford community. I believe that it is important to set a good example for young people, like my granddaughter to follow.

Military Service

I served many years in our country's military and know what it means to be a great citizen. It is our duty to pass on these values to our children for generations to come.

Tae-kwon-do

I believe it is important to stay active and exercise discipline, not only at my age but as children too. That is why I have taken it upon myself to learn tae-kwon-do.

Church involvement

I am an active member of Meridian Church of God, and I make an effort to serve and help others wherever possible.

Flood relief

In the aftermath of the bursting of Sanford dam, I was recognized as a local hero.

Teaching

I have many years' experience teaching children and adults both in and out of a public classroom setting. I believe it is important to help where you can.

Coaching

Good sportsmanship and a passion for teamwork are crucial life skills that I believe are important to instill in our future leaders will need both on and off the field.

Friendship

I believe that it is important to not only teach our children, but also let them know that they can count on us as a community to support them and encourage them wherever life takes them.

Parenting

I have watched two children and one grandchild grow up in Sanford. With the help of various local schools both of them have grown up to be successful and admirable individuals.
